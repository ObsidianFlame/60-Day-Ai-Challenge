What i built:
An 11-page Business Strategy Report and a single-page HTML visual dashboard, both generated programmatically. 
The report runs a validated startup idea through a full viability stress test: startup summary, business reality check, business model canvas, revenue and pricing strategy, go-to-market phases, customer acquisition strategy, first 100 users plan, competitive moat analysis, reverse SWOT, investor one-liner, 30-second pitch, founder action sheet, and a five-dimension investment scorecard. 
The PDF was built using Python and ReportLab. The dashboard was built in vanilla HTML and CSS — dark navy, teal accents, score bars, GTM funnel, moat layers, revenue projection, and a final verdict. 
The analysis returned a score of 64/100 and a verdict of VALIDATE.

What I learned:
The most useful section in the entire report was the first one: the Business Reality Check. Specifically the question "who pays?" — not who benefits, not who uses the product daily, but who actually controls the budget. 
In today's build, three entirely different people are involved in the same transaction. Separating them changed the go-to-market completely. The second learning was about trigger type. 
A trigger that drives purchase decisions can be proactive or reactive. If the trigger is episodic — something that happens to a customer once, gets resolved, and is forgotten — selling before the event is a fundamentally harder GTM challenge than selling after it. 
The financial model looked right. The score came back at 64. A model that looks right and a business that works are two different things, and the gap between them is a single paying customer.
