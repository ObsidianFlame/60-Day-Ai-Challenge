What I Built — Day 23

Today's build was a full Customer and MVP Blueprint — a structured consulting-grade framework that takes a business idea and forces it through 14 analytical sections before a single line of code is written. 
The output was an 8-page PDF report generated programmatically using Python and ReportLab, covering customer discovery, buyer personas, pain point mapping, feature prioritisation using MoSCoW, a pricing hypothesis, top five risks, a 30-day execution plan, and a single binary go/no-go validation signal. 
The report was clean enough to hand to a client, produced in under a session, and stripped of all branding to serve purely as a learning artefact.

What I learned

The startup validation report from Day 22 told us whether an idea was worth pursuing. Today made clear what that report cannot do on its own: it cannot tell you who to build for first, what the smallest viable proof of value looks like, or what a customer would actually pay before the product exists. 
Day 22 produces a verdict. Day 23 produces a plan. The sequence matters. Skipping straight from idea to build — which most founders do — means constructing a product on assumptions that have never been tested against a real person in a real commercial context. 
Yesterday's report scored the idea. Today's blueprint asked whether the customer would buy it.
