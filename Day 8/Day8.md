Claude Artifacts allow you to generate fully interactive applications instead of simple text outputs. By combining prompts, reasoning, design instructions, and structured requirements, Claude can build dashboards, tools, calculators, visualizations, and complete HTML applications.

What I did:
Today we built a full Personal Environmental Health Analyzer via the Claude AI coding challenge.
The project can be downloaded into a complete, interactive HTML file.
Today was combining multiple aspects of prompt engineering, including context- and Chain-of-Thought engineering.
The prompt included data rules, analysis, instructions for the dashboard, a personal report card and produced some incredible outputs, including insights and personalised recommendations.

What I learned:
The level of context you give the tool determines the 'complexity' of the output, but it's not only about what you want the output to be, but equally as important was what you want to exclude.
The outcomes of 'triaging' through colour coding, providing insights and daily actions to take was a learning curve i didn't know i needed, providing the baseline for more in-depth interactive analyzers going forward.

I used no coding whatsoever, only natural language to guide the AI on what i wanted, including the intentions. Even though this was from a prompt template, the outcome was an indication of the potential of this type of technology to break down previous barriers that would prevent most of us from building something incredible.
I think about the potential use cases, from Environmental Health to food health (macro tracking), to other analyzers like exercise and recovery. There is so much opportunity to improve the lives around us, add value to the world and transofrm our professions.

In utilising prompt engineering best practices, the output took a prolonged period to produce the dashboard, which i was very impressed with.
I learned that i need to continue iterating my personal prompting, as the more you use prompting, the better you get at it.
As healthcare leaders, we tend to focus on fighting fires, ensuring that organisations are making revenue, staff are coping and not burnt out, and patient complaints are managed timeously. All to often, we forget to take a wider view that objectively assesses our environment and identify other factors that affect health.

The most important learning from today is not 'which city is cleanest,' but how we are able to use AI to analyse variables beyond what we thought was possible.
I'm certainly looking forward to building interactive dashboards in my daily work and improving my current workflows, staying on top of things through interactive dashboards.
