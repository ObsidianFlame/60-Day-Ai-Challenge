Build an AI Shark Tank Simulator
Pitch your startup to AI investors

What I built:
A single-file AI Shark Tank Simulator that puts your startup idea in front of 5 distinct investor personas, each powered by Claude.
You enter your pitch across 6 fields. The tool generates 10 bespoke questions tuned to your specific idea — not generic startup questions, but questions that reference your revenue model, your market, your exact funding ask. 
Each judge has a defined personality (persona, no real actual people) and a defined blind spot. Marcus Chen only cares about TAM. Zara Nova will challenge your entire premise. David Hart wants the unit economics before anything else.

What i learned
I hit a CSS specificity bug that stopped the whole app from navigating between screens. It took two minutes to spot and one line to fix. 
But it made me honest about something: I build fast and I skip the edge cases. Moving fast is a strength, but not pressure-testing the corners is something I need to work out.
The more important lesson came from what this tool actually does. Any founder can now sit down, fill in six fields, and get ten sharp investor questions generated specifically for their idea. 
The questions about their revenue model, their specific market, their exact funding ask used to require knowing the right people or paying for a pitch coach. 
Now the barrier is the quality of your own thinking.
That said, I am clear about what this is not. A real pitch room has interruption, body language, silence, and rapport built over years. 
A judge who leans forward when you mention a competitor. One who checks out when you stumble on unit economics. Text on a screen cannot replicate that pressure. 
This tool sharpens your argument, but does not prepare you for the room. It does, however prepare you for the beginning of the process.
